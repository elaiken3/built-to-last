The Foundation

Core Principles

With EDS, your joints are already working overtime just to hold you upright. The goal here is to build enough muscle that your body doesn't have to brace to exist — without triggering the hyperdrive compensation patterns that cause neck tension, migraines, and flares.

What We Avoid vs. Prioritize

Avoid (for now)

  • Overhead press
  • Heavy farmer carries
  • KB swings (high rep)
  • Floor push-ups early
  • Upright rows / shrugs
  • Breath-holding
  • Looking up during lifts
  • Jaw clenching

Prioritize

  • Glute + leg hypertrophy
  • Supported pulling
  • Neck-safe pressing
  • Mid/lower trap work
  • Slow tempos
  • Breathing + rib position
  • Mid-range control
  • Stopping 1 rep early
12-Week Progression
Weeks 1–4
Foundation

RPE 6–7. Focus on form, joint tolerance, and consistency. Leave workouts feeling "worked" — not wrecked.

Weeks 5–8
Growth

Main lifts move to 4×6–10. Add 1 extra set to 1–2 exercises if recovery stays good.

Weeks 9–12
Strength

Squat/deadlift: 5×5–8. Press: 4×6–10. Accessories stay in 10–15 range. Never below 5 reps.

Daily Readiness

Green / Yellow / Red

Consistency beats hero workouts every time — especially with EDS. Use this system every session.

Green

Feeling good. Do the full plan as written.

Yellow

Fatigued or flare approaching. Cut all sets in half — 1–2 sets only.

Red

Active flare or pain. Skip to Flare Day plan: mobility + walk only.

Progression Rule

Double Progression

Work within the rep range (e.g., 8–12). Once you hit the top of the range on all sets with perfect form and slow tempo, increase weight next session — but only by 2.5–5 lbs total. Do not increase reps AND weight at the same time.

With EDS: progress only when all three are true — (1) full rep range achieved across all sets, (2) tempo stayed slow throughout, (3) no neck/trap takeover, and (4) no symptom spike the next day.

Warmup

8–10 minutes. Do this before every session — A, B, and C. It incorporates your existing PT movements and adds a few key additions for EDS safety.

Controlled squat to a chair or just below parallel. Knees track over toes, chest up. These are your PT squats — same movement.

Your PT bridges. Drive hips up, squeeze glutes for 1 second, lower slowly. Ribs down — don't overextend at the top.

3 Cat-Cow 6 reps (slow)

On hands and knees. Inhale as you arch (cow), exhale as you round (cat). Move through the full spine — don't rush. This is a nervous system reset as much as a mobility drill.

4 Wall Slides 8 reps

Stand with back against a wall, elbows and wrists in contact. Slide arms up slowly, keeping contact the whole way. Scapulae draw down and back — not shrugging. Critical for EDS shoulder patterning.

5 Hip Hinge Drill 8 reps

Stand with hands on thighs. Push hips back as hands slide toward knees — spine stays neutral. This grooves the pattern for your RDL and KB deadlift before any weight is added.

6 Chin Tuck + Nods 6 gentle reps

Stand or sit tall. Gently draw chin straight back (not down). From there, small slow nod. This organizes the neck before loading and significantly reduces trap compensation and migraine risk during your workout.

7 Scapular Set + Breath 5 slow breaths

Stand tall. Draw shoulder blades gently down and back — hold that position. Take 5 slow belly breaths without losing the scapular position. This is your "ready state" before every set.

Squat + Glutes + Supported Upper

Day A

Lower-body squat emphasis + supported upper body. Tempo: 3 sec down · 1 sec pause · smooth up. Do not lock joints at top of any movement.

1 Goblet Squat to Chair / Box 3 × 6–10
Feel it: Quads + Glutes · Rest: 90 sec

Hold one DB or KB vertically at your chest. Squat to a chair or box to control depth. Stand without snapping knees at the top.

  • Ribs stacked over hips
  • Knees track over toes — no caving
  • Sit down under full control (3 sec)
  • Soft knees at the top — no lockout
Watch Tutorial
2 Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift 3 × 6–10
Feel it: Hamstrings + Glutes · Rest: 90 sec

Hold DBs in front of thighs. Hinge at the hips — knees soft, hips travel back, DBs slide down your legs. Stop at the hamstring stretch, not the floor.

  • Hips back — not "bend over"
  • Neutral spine — don't look up
  • Stop when you feel hamstring load
  • EDS note: shorten range if you're hanging on joints
⚠ Stop if: you feel low back taking over instead of hamstrings.
Watch Tutorial
3 Supported 1-Arm DB Row 3 × 8–12 / side
Feel it: Mid-back + Lats · Rest: 60 sec

Hand and knee supported on a chair or couch. Torso parallel to floor. Row the DB toward your hip — not your armpit. Pause 1 second at the top.

  • Shoulder stays away from ear — not shrugging
  • Elbow to back pocket (not ceiling)
  • 1-sec squeeze at top
  • Slow lower — 3 seconds
⚠ Stop if: neck tightens or you start shrugging.
Watch Tutorial
4 DB Floor Press (Neutral Grip) 3 × 8–12
Feel it: Chest + Triceps · Rest: 90 sec

Lie on the floor with DBs, palms facing each other (neutral grip). Elbows at ~45°. Press up, stop just short of lockout. Neutral grip significantly reduces shoulder irritation.

  • Head fully relaxed on floor
  • Ribs down — exhale as you press
  • Elbows not flared (45° angle)
  • No rib flare at top
Watch Tutorial
5 Step-Ups (Low Step) 2 × 8–10 / side
Feel it: Glutes + Quads · Rest: 60 sec

Use a low step or sturdy surface. Step up slowly, trailing leg barely touches. Step down under control. No pushing off the trailing leg — all work goes to the standing leg.

  • Light tap with trailing foot — no push off
  • Knee tracks straight
  • Go slow — especially down
  • No knee snap at the top
Watch Tutorial
6 Dead Bug / Heel Slides 2–3 × 6 / side
Feel it: Deep core · Rest: 30 sec

On your back. Gently press lower back into floor. Lower one leg (straight) or slide heel out while extending opposite arm. Slow and deliberate.

  • Low back gently presses into floor
  • Exhale fully on each rep
  • Move slow — this is not a speed drill
⚠ Stop immediately if: you feel abdominal "doming" or coning.

Hinge + Single Leg + Upper Back

Day B

Hip hinge emphasis, single-leg work, and posture-focused upper back. Tempo: 3 sec down · 1 sec pause · smooth up. Take the support on single-leg work — EDS joints benefit from it.

1 KB Deadlift (Elevated if Needed) 3 × 6–10
Feel it: Glutes + Hamstrings · Rest: 90 sec

KB on the floor between feet. If floor range is too much, elevate it on books or a step. Hinge down, grip, pull shoulders back without shrugging, then "push the floor away."

  • Wedge: pull shoulder blades down/back — no shrug
  • Push the floor away (not "pick up" the KB)
  • Stand tall — don't lean back at the top
  • Soft knees — no lockout
Watch Tutorial
2 Split Squat (Short Range + Support) 3 × 6–10 / side
Feel it: Front glute + quad · Rest: 60–90 sec

Staggered stance. Use a chair or wall for hand support — this is not cheating, it's smart with EDS. Shorten the range of motion at first. Control > depth.

  • Stay tall — don't dump forward
  • Short range is fine — build gradually
  • Front knee tracks straight
  • Hold support for joint stability
⚠ Stop if: knee wobbles uncontrollably or hip drops to one side.
Watch Tutorial
3 Chest-Supported DB Row 3 × 10–12
Feel it: Upper/mid back — not traps · Rest: 60 sec

Lie prone on a bench (or incline chair). Let arms hang, then row elbows back. Chest stays heavy on support throughout — this removes spinal load and trap compensation almost entirely.

  • Chest stays heavy on bench — no lift-off
  • Pull elbows BACK — not up
  • Wide collarbones (not pinched forward)
  • No shrugging — shoulders stay away from ears

No bench? Use the supported 1-arm row from Day A instead, keeping weight light and strict.

Watch Tutorial
4 Incline DB Press or Elevated Push-Up 2–3 × 8–12
Feel it: Chest — neck relaxed · Rest: 90 sec

Incline DB press is generally more shoulder-friendly than flat. Elevated push-up (hands on a couch or table) reduces ground-level neck strain. Choose based on how shoulders feel that day.

  • Long neck — no chin jutting forward
  • Shoulders down and back
  • Ribs don't flare at the top
  • Stop 1–2 reps early if tension builds
⚠ Skip this exercise entirely on yellow or red days — it's the most migraine-sensitive movement in the program.
Watch Tutorial
5 Weighted Glute Bridge / Hip Thrust 3 × 10–15
Feel it: Glutes (not low back) · Rest: 60 sec

On the floor, DB or KB resting on your hips. Drive hips up, squeeze glutes hard for 1 second. Strong glutes reduce spinal load across everything else you do.

  • Ribs down — posterior pelvic tilt
  • Belt buckle pointing up at the top
  • 1-sec squeeze at top
  • No rib flare — don't overextend
⚠ Stop if: you feel it in your low back instead of glutes. Reduce weight and reset rib position.
Watch Tutorial
6 Reverse Fly or Band Pull-Aparts 2 × 12–15
Feel it: Rear shoulders + mid-back · Rest: 30 sec

Keep the weight very light — lighter than feels necessary. These are not a strength exercise; they're building the posture architecture that keeps your neck safe.

  • Arms long, very light weight
  • Wide collarbones — not pinched
  • Shoulders in back pockets — stay down
  • Move slow
⚠ If traps fire immediately: reduce weight by half and try again.

Red Day Plan

Flare Day

Use this when: a flare is coming, migraine risk is high, sleep was poor, joints are unstable, or energy is low. The goal is to stay consistent — not to train through symptoms. 10–15 minutes total.

Breath Reset — 2 minutes

Lie on your back. Place hands on your ribs. Take 6 slow exhales, shoulders heavy, jaw unclenched. This resets your nervous system before you move.

2 Rounds — Easy and Slow
Glute bridge× 10
Box squat (partial range)× 8
Supported row — very light× 10 / side
Dead bug or heel slides× 5 / side

Walk: 5–15 minutes if possible. Gentle pace. This is enough. If you finish feeling better than you started, it was perfect.

Neck Tightness Rescue

If traps or neck start firing during any regular workout — stop. Use this before deciding whether to continue or call it a day.

  • Put the weight down — no exceptions
  • 3 slow exhales — shoulders drop on each one
  • Chin tuck + gentle nod — 3 reps
  • Reassess: reduce weight by 10–20% and restart
Stop the session entirely if you feel: neck pain building during a set · tingling down an arm · migraine aura or pressure · shoulder pinching at the front of the shoulder joint.

Starting Point

How to Choose Your Weight

With EDS, choose weights based on stability first — not strength. The weight that feels too easy at rep 1 might become the right weight by rep 8 when you're going slow and feeling every inch of the movement.

1

The "Perfect 8" Test

Pick a weight. Do 8 reps with full 3-second tempo. Reps 1–6 should feel smooth. Reps 7–8 should be harder but still stable — no shaking, no neck tension, no joint wobble. You should feel like you could do 3–4 more if forced.

2

If 8 Feels Too Easy

You could do 12+ comfortably → go heavier next set. But verify: was the tempo actually slow? Were joints actually stable? Many people discover the weight was "right" once tempo is honest.

3

If Compensation Appears

Neck tension, shrugging, wobbling, jaw clenching → weight is too heavy. Reduce by 10–20% and retest. This is not failure — this is accurate training.

Progression

When to Add Weight

Progress only when all four are true: (1) you hit the top of the rep range on all sets, (2) tempo stayed slow throughout, (3) no traps or neck took over, (4) no symptom spike the next day.

When those are all true → add 2.5–5 lbs total (not per hand) OR add 1–2 reps. Never increase both at once. For lower body, you can progress slightly faster than upper body.

Troubleshooting

Problem Traps or neck engaging
  • Reduce weight 10–20%
  • Add 1 second pause at the controlled position
  • Keep chin gently tucked
  • Shorten the set — stop 2 reps earlier
Problem Joint wobble or instability
  • Go lighter immediately
  • Add physical support (wall, chair, bench)
  • Shorten range of motion
  • Slow the tempo down further
Goal The right weight feels like
  • Fully controlled across all reps
  • Working muscles are fatiguing — not joints
  • Neck and traps stay quiet
  • You feel worked — not wrecked
